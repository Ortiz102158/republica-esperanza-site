People’s Republic of Hope

A project of Popular Sovereignty, Anti-Imperialist Resistance, and Solidarity

The People’s Republic of Hope is not a utopia — it is the organized will of the Dominican people to break chains of foreign domination, reject odious debt, and build a future based on popular sovereignty, solidarity economy, and anti-imperialist unity with the peoples of Latin America and the Caribbean.

We affirm that true human rights cannot exist under foreign financial control or militarized borders. Our struggle centers on the rights of workers, peasants, women, youth, migrants, and Afro-descendant and Indigenous communities — not as individuals to be “protected” by the state, but as collective subjects of history.

Justice means dismantling the carceral system, ending U.S. military presence, and reclaiming sovereignty over our land, water, and seeds.

The Dominican Republic will never be free while it remains a financial colony of the IMF, World Bank, and U.S. capital. We demand:

  • Cancellation of all odious debt imposed by imperialist institutions;
  • Expulsion of U.S. Southern Command and all foreign military bases;
  • Rejoining ALBA-TCP and forging integration with Cuba, Venezuela, and Haiti — not through charity, but through solidarity, mutual aid, and shared resistance.

Featured: Dominican Tobacco — Sovereignty in Every Leaf

By Ing. Francisco Matos

Far from a commodity for export, Dominican tobacco carries centuries of peasant resistance — from Taíno cultivation to the hands of small farmers defying agribusiness monopolies. Reclaiming tobacco as a national strategic resource means ending corporate land grabs, restoring cooperatives, and building a value chain under popular control — not foreign certification.
